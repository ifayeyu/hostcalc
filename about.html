About HostCalc

A free Airbnb income calculator built to give STR hosts a realistic picture of their net earnings — not just gross revenue.

What Is HostCalc?

HostCalc is a free, browser-based Airbnb income calculator and short-term rental (STR) profitability dashboard. It was built to answer the question that every prospective Airbnb host asks — and that few tools answer honestly: how much will I actually make?

Most Airbnb income estimates start and stop at nightly rate × occupancy. That number looks great on paper. HostCalc goes further, helping you model all the costs that reduce it: Airbnb's host service fee, cleaning costs, supplies, property costs (mortgage or rent), utilities, maintenance reserves, insurance, local taxes, and management fees if you use a property manager. The result is your projected net income — the number that actually matters.

No account, no data stored: HostCalc runs entirely in your browser. Your property details, nightly rates, and financial inputs are never sent to a server. Everything is calculated locally and is gone when you close the tab.

Who HostCalc Is Built For

HostCalc is designed for three kinds of users:

  • Prospective hosts evaluating whether to list a property on Airbnb — use HostCalc to model different nightly rates, occupancy scenarios, and cost structures before committing
  • Active Airbnb hosts who want to understand exactly where their revenue is going and identify which costs are eating the most into their margins
  • Real estate investors analyzing short-term rental potential for a property they're considering purchasing — model ROI scenarios across different markets and price points

HostCalc works for any property type — private rooms, entire apartments, vacation homes, and multi-property portfolios. The dashboard lets you model your numbers quickly and export a summary for your records.

Why Gross Revenue Is Misleading

The short-term rental industry has a persistent problem: most income projections wildly overstate what hosts actually take home. "You could earn $4,000 a month!" sounds great until you subtract the platform fees, cleaning costs after every turnover, supplies, utilities, insurance, and the 2 AM toilet repair that wasn't in anyone's spreadsheet.

HostCalc was built specifically to counter this. By modeling all the real costs — not just the optimistic headline revenue — hosts can make informed decisions about pricing, listing strategy, and whether STR hosting is actually financially worthwhile for their specific situation and property.

The typical Airbnb host in a competitive urban market takes home 50–65% of their gross booking revenue after platform fees and operating costs. In less competitive or seasonal markets, this number can be lower. Understanding your real margin before you start — not after you've been hosting for six months — is the entire point of HostCalc.

How HostCalc Calculates Income

HostCalc uses the same methodology professional STR analysts and real estate investors use to evaluate short-term rental properties:

  • Gross Revenue = Nightly Rate × Nights Occupied (based on your projected occupancy rate)
  • Platform Fee = Airbnb's host service fee (3% for most listings using the split-fee structure)
  • Net Revenue = Gross Revenue − Platform Fee
  • Operating Expenses = Cleaning + Supplies + Utilities + Maintenance Reserve + Insurance + Management Fee
  • Net Operating Income (NOI) = Net Revenue − Operating Expenses
  • Net Income = NOI − Property Costs (mortgage or rent payment)

Tax treatment is complex and jurisdiction-specific, so HostCalc presents pre-tax net income. Always consult a tax professional to understand how your STR income will be taxed in your specific situation.
