Shared Hosting Cost 2026:
What to Expect & Best Value

Shared hosting ranges from $1–$10/month — but what you actually pay depends on intro pricing, renewal rates, and what's included. Here's the full breakdown.

How Much Does Shared Hosting Cost in 2026?

Shared hosting in 2026 typically costs $2–$10 per month for standard plans, with budget providers starting as low as $1/month on promotional pricing. The wide range comes down to three factors: the provider, your contract length, and whether you're seeing the intro rate or the renewal rate.

What's Included in Shared Hosting?

Most shared hosting plans at the $2–$5/month level include:

  • 1–unlimited websites (depending on tier)
  • Free SSL certificate (HTTPS)
  • Free domain name for the first year (check renewal cost — usually $12–$18/yr)
  • 1-click WordPress installation
  • Email hosting (webmail access)
  • Basic cPanel or custom dashboard
  • Unmetered or limited storage (often SSD)

What Shared Hosting Is Good For

✅ Right fit for shared hosting:

  • Personal websites, portfolios, and blogs
  • Small business sites with under 10,000 monthly visitors
  • New sites still building traffic
  • Side projects and experimental sites
  • Clients who want a simple online presence

❌ When to skip shared hosting:

  • E-commerce sites with significant transaction volume (performance matters)
  • Sites that regularly exceed 50,000 monthly visitors
  • Applications requiring custom server configuration
  • Sites with strict uptime or speed SLAs

Hidden Costs to Watch For

The advertised monthly price is rarely the whole story. Watch for these add-ons:

  • Domain renewal — Free first year, then $12–$18/yr. Factor this into your annual cost.
  • SSL certificate — Usually free via Let's Encrypt, but some hosts charge $60+/yr for premium SSLs
  • Backups — Automated daily backups often cost $2–$4/mo extra
  • Site migration — Moving from another host can cost $30–$150 if you need help
  • Email storage — Some plans limit mailbox storage; upgrades cost extra

How to Calculate Your True Hosting Cost

Use this formula to compare providers on equal footing:

True 2-year cost = (Intro price × 12 months) + (Renewal price × 12 months) + Domain renewal ($15) + any required add-ons

Example — Bluehost Basic: ($2.95 × 12) + ($10.99 × 12) + $15 = $35.40 + $131.88 + $15 = $182.28 over 2 years

Example — Namecheap: ($1.58 × 12) + ($4.98 × 12) + $15 = $18.96 + $59.76 + $15 = $93.72 over 2 years
