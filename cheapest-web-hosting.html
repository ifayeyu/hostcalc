What to Watch Out For With Cheap Hosting

Performance on shared servers

The cheapest plans put hundreds of websites on the same server. During traffic spikes on other sites, yours slows down. For most small sites this is invisible, but if you expect viral moments or large traffic events, shared hosting can fail you.

Storage and bandwidth limits

"Unlimited" storage is a marketing term — most hosts have fair use policies that cap you at 10–50GB in practice. For sites with lots of images or video, verify actual storage limits before signing up.

Email reliability

Cheap shared hosting often means your email shares an IP with spammers. If professional email reliability matters to you, use Google Workspace ($6/month) or Zoho Mail alongside your cheap hosting plan.

Bottom line on "cheap" hosting: Always lock in the longest term you're comfortable with to maximize the intro rate. Calculate renewal costs before committing. And read the cancellation policy — refund windows are usually 30 days.

Free Alternatives to Consider

For very basic needs, free hosting options exist — though with significant limitations:

  • Cloudflare Pages — Free static site hosting, ideal for HTML/CSS/JS sites with no backend
  • GitHub Pages — Free for public repositories, good for developer portfolios and documentation
  • Netlify (free tier) — Free for static sites with 100GB bandwidth/month

These are genuinely free with no catch for simple sites. The trade-off is no database, no server-side code, and limited support. WordPress and dynamic sites require paid hosting.
